About Me

I’ve always been full of questions. I have a fundamental need to find out why and how things work, and I see these inner machinations as opportunities to make them better. My curiosity and desire to improve things has led me to the Management Engineering program at the University of Waterloo, a hybrid industrial engineering and software program. I hope to apply the skills I learn to improve every system, no matter how big or small and hopefully make the world a better place.

In my spare time I love watching the Blue Jays and modding my darling metallic grey Mazda 3, as well as consuming a little too much Netflix.

Software Engineering Intern

Technologies/Tools Used: Java, Python, .NET, SQL, VBA, UIPath, Git, JIRA


Capgemini was my introduction to proper software development in an agile space and it made me realize how much more efficient some of my earlier teams could have been. It was also my first opportunity to work in a truly massive company with more than a 100,000 people employed world wide.

I had the opportunity to work with some extremely talented people and had a ton of fun working in downtown Toronto. While I was there, I worked on a lot of RPA 'robots', which are essentially automated programs that can replace team members. I also had the opportunity to present to the CEO of Capgemini Canada on the potential of AI in RPA and how that had the ability to automate tasks that previously would have been thought to be too judgement based.
